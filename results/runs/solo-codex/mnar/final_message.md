Main findings: the dataset is structurally clean, but `reported_annual_income` is missing for 49.5% of rows, and that missingness is associated with age, education, and satisfaction, so complete-case income analyses are not safely MCAR. Among reported incomes, age and education are the strongest positive correlates; `num_children` is modestly negative. Satisfaction is only weakly explained by the available features: even with income imputation and a missingness indicator, cross-validated models had near-zero or negative `R^2`, so there is little predictive signal there.

Validation was included: residual diagnostics, Breusch-Pagan tests, Shapiro checks, and 5-fold cross-validation for linear and random-forest baselines. The income model had modest explanatory power (`R^2` about 0.21 in-sample; CV `R^2` about 0.16), while the satisfaction models did not generalize meaningfully. No extra packages were needed.
