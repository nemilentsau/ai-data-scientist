The main result is that `severity_index` drives the dataset: it strongly predicts longer stays and worse recovery. Treatment groups are materially confounded at baseline, so raw comparisons are misleading; after adjustment, treatment `B` is associated with about a 4.72-point lower recovery score than `A`, while length-of-stay differences mostly disappear once severity is included. `readmitted` does not appear meaningfully predictable from the available features, with cross-validated classifier performance near chance.
