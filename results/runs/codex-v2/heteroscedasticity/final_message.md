Main result: this dataset behaves like a very regular campaign-level marketing table where revenue is explained mostly by spend, not by channel or season. The strongest findings were a near-linear spend-to-revenue relationship (`R^2 = 0.945`, slope about 2.49 revenue dollars per 1 spend dollar), no meaningful ROAS differences by channel or month, and more variability in the impression-to-click step than in the spend-to-revenue step.
